  • 7. What are the reflections of the parietal pleura and the lungs

  • 9. Know the difference between the typical/atypical and true/false ribs.
  • 10. When does the manubrusternal joint fuse/ossify

ANSWERS

  • 1. Rib 7, T3, rib 2
  • 2. Forms synovial joints with T4 and T5 vertebrae
    • ...Facet on the tubercle of 5th rib forms a synovial joint with the facet on the transverse process of T5 vertebra
    • ...Superior articular facet on the head of 5th rib forms a synovial joint with the inferior articular facet on the body of T4 vertebra
    • ...Inferior articular facet on the head of 5th rib forms a synovial joint with the superior articular facet on the body of T5 vertebra.
  • 3. Rib 1 - single articular facet for synovial joint with upper hemifacet on lateral body of T1.
  • 4. Intercostal veins, arteries and nerves in the order VAN( vein, artery, nerve )
  • 5. Parietal and visceral pleura, the lungs, spleen
  • 6. Oblique fissure- posteriorly- 2nd rib, anteriorly- 6th rib. Horizontal fissure- midaxillary – 5th and anteriorly – 4th rib.
  • 7. Parietal pleura , midclavicular – 8th rib , midaxillary – 10th rib , posteriorly – 12th rib
    ....Lungs, midclavicular – 6th rib , midaxillary – 8th rib , posteriorly – 10th rib
  • 8. a = 2nd, b = 5th
  • 9. typical ribs = ribs 3 – 9
    ...atypical = ribs 1,2,10-12
    ...true ribs = ribs 1 – 7
    ...false ribs = ribs 8-10
    ...floating ribs = 11 and 12
    ...Ribs are classified as true when they articulate directly with the sternum( via their cartilages)
  • 10. between 50 – 60 years
