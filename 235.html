ANSWERS

  • 1. 4 – 6 months
  • 2. There are two bursae at the shoulder(glenohumeral joint), the subtendinous joint is the smaller. The subacromial bursa is the nore prominent and is responsible for most of the movement of the shoulder joint
  • 3. The capitate
  • 4. triquetrium
  • 5. Scaphoid, Lunate
  • 6. Arrangement, Pnemonic to help remember, Some Lovers Try Positions That They Cant handle…
    • S – Scaphoid
    • L – Lunate
    • T – Triquetium
    • P – Pisiform
    • Trapezium
    • T – Trapezoid
    • C – Capitate
    • H – Hamate
    • Arrangement is from lateral to medial in the proximal row and then in the distal row
  • 7. Trapezoid and Lunate
  • 8. Radial artery, ulnar artery
  • 9. Flexed – angle of a quadrilateral triangle, extended – straight
  • 10. Arterioles
  • Gomphosis
  • 12. A birth defect characterized by lessed developed or absent pectoralis muscle on one side of the body
  • 13. Median nerve originates from C5 to T1 while the medial root of the median nerve originates from C8 and T1.
  • 14. The tone of the muscles around the joint
  • 15. Flexion – Abduction – Extension – Adduction
  • 16. The subtendinous bursa and subacromial bursa
  • 17. It is a small fluid filled sac lined by synovial fluid
  • 18. Muscle spindles – respond to stretch, tendinous organs – respond to tension
  • 19. It protects the glenohumural joint against superior dislocation
  • 20. C8 and T1
  • 21. The carpal bones and the flexor retinaculum
  • 22. Tendons of 9 flexor muscles (flexor pollicis longus, the four tendons of the flexor digitorum superficialis and the four tendons of the flexor digitorum profundus) and the median nerve
  • 23. It is the compression of the median nerve, may be due to the collapse of any of the structures that pass through of form the tunnel
  • 24. Ulnar nerve
  • 25. Palmaris longus
  • 26. Trapezius muscle
  • 27. Radial artery
